1. Data Controller

The data controller for Mandala is:

  • Name / Legal entity: Nicolò Meduri, operating as Divora
  • Email: [email protected]

For any privacy-related request, please use the contact details above or reach us via the game's Steam store page.

Mandala is built with Unity and uses GameAnalytics to collect pseudonymous gameplay data for the sole purpose of improving the game.

2. Data We Collect

Mandala collects pseudonymous data through GameAnalytics. The data is not directly linked to a name or email address, but it may constitute personal data under the GDPR (Regulation (EU) 2016/679) because it includes persistent identifiers and technical attributes that could, in combination, relate to an identifiable natural person. Accordingly, this policy and the rights described herein apply in full.

2.1 - Device and Technical Data

  • Operating system and version
  • Device model and manufacturer
  • Game version installed
  • Network connection type (not content)
  • Pseudonymous user identifier assigned by GameAnalytics (a persistent ID not directly linkable to a real-world identity, but treated as personal data under GDPR)

2.2 - Session and Gameplay Data

  • Number of runs per session
  • Survival time per run
  • Distance traveled by the character
  • Number of pauses
  • Lowest FPS recorded (performance monitoring)
  • Best score achieved in the session
  • Total app session duration
  • Score snapshots over time (every 30 seconds)

2.3 - Combat and Progression Data

  • Enemy types and kill counts
  • Charged attacks used and cancelled
  • Projectiles fired, hits, and misses (accuracy rate)
  • Shield blocks
  • Enemy type that caused the player's death
  • Number of active enemies and spawn interval at time of death

2.4 - Upgrade and Powerup System Data

  • Powerups offered per type
  • Powerups selected per type
  • Number of upgrade rounds completed
  • Upgrade points earned
  • Average decision time in the selection screen
  • Maximum upgrade level reached
  • Final build composition at run end

2.5 - Behavioural Data

  • Pickups collected per type
  • Input method used (keyboard, controller, touch)
  • Quit context (pause menu, death screen, session end)
  • First-time player flag (anonymous boolean)

Providing this data is a technical consequence of playing the game. No separate action is required from the user to transmit this data. The analytics collection cannot be selectively disabled without affecting the use of the game.

3. Purpose of Processing

All collected data is used exclusively to:

  • Improve game balance and design (death patterns, progression, combat mechanics)
  • Optimise technical performance across hardware configurations
  • Understand usage patterns to prioritise future updates and content
  • Detect and fix bugs or gameplay anomalies

Data is never used for advertising, commercial profiling, or sold to third parties.

4. Legal Basis

The processing of pseudonymous analytics data is based on the legitimate interest of the data controller pursuant to Article 6(1)(f) of the GDPR.

The specific legitimate interests pursued are: improving the quality, stability, and balance of the game software; diagnosing technical issues; and understanding how players interact with the game in order to prioritise development resources.

A balancing assessment has been carried out. The data collected is limited in scope (gameplay metrics only), is pseudonymous, carries no financial or sensitive dimension, and cannot reasonably be expected to cause significant harm to users. Users retain the right to object to this processing at any time (see Section 9).

Where required by applicable law or where the processing is not covered by the legitimate interest basis, explicit user consent will be requested prior to data collection.

You may obtain further information about the safeguards in place, including a copy of the relevant SCCs, by contacting us at the address listed in Section 14.

8. Data Retention

Pseudonymous analytics data is retained on GameAnalytics servers for no longer than 24 months from the date of collection, after which it is deleted or further anonymised. This retention period is necessary for the statistical and longitudinal analysis required to improve game design and diagnose technical issues.

Once data is fully anonymised (i.e., no longer capable of identifying a natural person even indirectly), GDPR retention limits cease to apply to that data.

9. Your Rights (GDPR)

Because the data collected constitutes personal data under the GDPR, you have the following rights under Articles 15-22 GDPR:

  • Access (Art. 15) - request information about the data being processed about you
  • Rectification (Art. 16) - request correction of inaccurate data
  • Erasure / "Right to be forgotten" (Art. 17) - request removal of your data
  • Restriction of processing (Art. 18) - request limitation of processing in certain circumstances
  • Objection (Art. 21) - object to processing based on legitimate interest; we will cease processing unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests
  • Data portability (Art. 20) - receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format where technically feasible

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at: [email protected]

Please include in your request: a description of the right you wish to exercise, and any information that may help us identify your data (e.g., device type, game version, approximate dates of play). We will respond within 30 days of receipt.

Note: because the data is linked to a pseudonymous identifier rather than directly to your name or email, it may not always be technically possible to locate data relating to a specific individual. In such cases, we will inform you in accordance with Article 11(2) GDPR. This limitation does not, however, eliminate your rights or our obligations under the GDPR.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the competent supervisory authority. For users based in Italy: Garante per la Protezione dei Dati Personali - garanteprivacy.it

10. Children

Mandala may be played by users of all ages, including children. The analytics data collected through GameAnalytics (described in Section 2) applies equally to all players regardless of age. The data is limited to pseudonymous gameplay metrics, contains no sensitive information, and is used solely to improve the game. No name, email address, location, or any directly identifying information is collected from any user, including minors.

Account creation and age verification on Steam are managed by Valve Corporation. Parents and guardians are encouraged to use Steam's Family View and parental control features to manage their child's activity on the platform.

If you are a parent or guardian and wish to request deletion of data potentially relating to your child, please contact us at divoragg@gmail.com. Note that because data is pseudonymous and not linked to a name or account, identification of specific records may not always be technically possible.

11. Automated Decision-Making and Profiling

Mandala does not carry out any automated decision-making or profiling within the meaning of Article 22 GDPR. The analytics data collected is used solely for aggregate statistical analysis by the developer and is not used to make decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects on individual users.
